    • Apr 26 2023

      Dhilan Lahoti

    We will start by discussing various definitions of Thom spectra and how they relate to one another. We will then show the equivalene of the two statements of Hopkins-Mahowald before discussing the proof of the theorem, which uses Steinberger's computation of the Dyer-Lashof operations on the dual Steenrod algebra. If time permits, we will see how this computation relates to Bökstedt's computation of \(\operatorname{THH}(\mathbb{F}_p)\).

    References:

    • Units of ring spectra and Thom spectra by Ando, Blumberg, Gepner, Hopkins, and Rezk for the different notions of Thom spectra.
    • A simple universal property of Thom ring spectra by Antolín-Camarena and Barthel for the universal property ofThom spectra.
    • On a nilpotence conjecture of J.P. May by Mathew, Naumann, and Noel for the Hopkins-Mahowald Theorem.
    • Homology Operations for \(H_\infty\) and \(H_n\) Ring Spectra by Steinberger for the action of the Dyer=Lashof operations on the dual Steenrod algebra.
    • Apr 19 2023

      Natalie Stewart

    We will begin by computing the \(\mathbb{F}_2\) cohomology of \(\mathrm{ko}\), which turns out to be \(H^*(\mathrm{ko}) \simeq \mathcal{A} \otimes_{\mathcal{A}(1)} \mathbb{F_2} \), for \(\mathcal{A}(1) = \mathcal{A}\langle \mathrm{Sq}^1, \mathrm{Sq}^2\rangle\) a finitely generated Steenrod subalgebra. By the free-forgetful adjunction, this implies that the \(E_2\)-page for the \(\mathrm{ko}\)-ASSeq is \(\mathrm{Ext}^{s,t}_{\mathcal{A}(1)} (\mathbb{F}_2,\mathbb{F}_2)\).

    Using this, we will characterize this \(E_2\)-page using a minimal \(\mathcal{A}(1)\)-resolution and a periodic \(\mathcal{A}(1)\)-resolution. After a short argument, we see that the \(\mathrm{ko}\)-ASSeq collapses at the \(E_2\) page. Along the way, we'll perform the computation pictured on the shirt from Paul Goerss's retirement conference.

    References:

    • Topological \(K\)-theory, by Rognes for an account including Stong's computation of \(H^*(\mathrm{ko})\) and using the Cartan-Eilenberg spectral sequence to conclude, with some computational holes.
    • The green book ch. 3 by Ravanel for a quick, painful, and more-or-less complete account for \(\mathrm{bo} = \tau_{> 0} \mathrm{ko}\) using the Cartan-Eilenberg spectral sequence.
    • A guide for computing stable homotopy groups, by Beaudry & Campbell for something approachable, including minimal resolutions.

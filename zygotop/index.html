    • Oct 25 2023

      Isabel Longbottom

    We give an introduction to algebraic K-theory, beginning with definitions and building up basic and not-so-basic properties. We will touch on connective vs nonconnective K-theory, explain why it is difficult to compute, and survey some recent advances. We will be motivated by the computation of \(K(\mathbb{F}_q)\), but do some sightseeing along the way.

    • Nov 08 2023

      Natalia Pacheco-Tallaj

    I will sketch the physical POV and the mathematical definition of the map \(\mathrm{TMF} \rightarrow \mathrm{KO}((q))\), and thereby attempt to convince you that ideas from physics can predict interesting mathematical results. I'll introduce the necessary physics and assume familiarity with TMF only up to the current point in Stephens topics course.

This is going to be a short post. Arun Debray motivated me to write this down.

Adams (and Toda) constructed an infinite family $$\alpha_i\in
\pi_{2i(p-1)-1}(\S_{(p)})$$. It was later proved (I don't know by whom) that
these elements all live on the $$3$$-sphere (localized at $$p$$): $$\alpha_i\in
\pi_{2i(p-1)+2}(S^3_{(p)})$$. For instance, $$\alpha_1: S^{2p} \to S^3$$ is the
first attaching map in $$\CP^p_{(p)}$$.  There's a simple construction of this
family that I'd like to decribe today.

Let's $$p$$-localize all our spaces and maps. Consider the unstable map
$$\alpha_1: S^{2p} \to S^3$$. This defines a map $$S^{2p-2} \to \Omega^2 S^3$$,
and hence an $$\E_1$$-map $$\Omega S^{2p-1} \to \Omega^2 S^3$$. By adjunction,
we obtain a map $$\Sigma \Omega S^{2p-1} \to \Omega S^3$$. The James splitting
gives an equivalence 

$$\Sigma \Omega S^{2p-1} \simeq \bigvee_{n\geq 0} \Sigma S^{2n(p-1)},$$

and so we obtain maps $$\Sigma S^{2n(p-1)} \to \Omega S^3$$. By adjunction, this
gives a map $$S^{2n(p-1)} \to \Omega^2 S^3$$, i.e., an element of
$$\pi_{2n(p-1)+ 2}(S^3)$$. This element is the unstable map $$\alpha_n$$.

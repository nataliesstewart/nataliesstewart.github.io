Just wanted to post two cute proofs that I came up with for the following
statement: the $$p$$-localization of $$\HP^\infty$$ is not a H-space for any
$$p\geq 3$$.

Here's one proof. We know that $$\H^\ast(\HP^\infty;\FF_p) \cong
\FF_p[x_4]$$, and $$P^1(x_4) = 2x_4^{(p+1)/2}$$. So, if $$f:\HP^\infty\to
\HP^\infty$$ is such that $$f(x_4) = nx_4$$, then we have

$$2nx_4^{(p+1)/2} = f(P^1(x_4)) = P^1(f(x_4)) = 2(nx_4)^{(p+1)/2} \pmod{p},$$

so $$n\equiv n^{(p+1)/2} \pmod{p}$$. But if $$\HP^\infty$$ was a H-space, we'd
have a self-map $$f$$ such that $$f(x_4) = nx_4$$: namely, the $$n$$th power map
$$\HP^\infty \to \HP^\infty$$. So this congruence would have to hold for all
integers $$n$$, and that's a problem.

Here is a more complicated argument. We know that all Whitehead products vanish
in H-spaces, so it suffices to find a nontrivial Whitehead product in $$\pi_\ast
\HP^\infty$$ (again, $$p$$-localized). Consider the generator $$\iota: \HP^1 =
S^4 \hookrightarrow \HP^\infty$$. Then, the $$(p+1)/2$$-fold Whitehead product
$$[\iota, \cdots, \iota]$$ lives in $$\pi_{2p+1} \HP^\infty$$. This Whitehead
product is $$p$$-torsion: in fact, under the isomorphism $$\pi_{2p+1} \HP^\infty
\cong \pi_{2p}(S^3)$$, this Whitehead product is just $$\alpha_1$$.

I was going to write a post about nonabelian Hodge theory, but since I've
already written up notes, I figured that it might just be better to link to it.
[Here](http://www.mit.edu/~sanathd/nah.pdf) are my notes (might have typos), and
[here](http://www.mit.edu/~sanathd/slides-nah.pdf) are slides. I just want to
ramble about a vague philosophy which I think nonabelian Hodge theory
encapsulates well.

The general philosophy behind nonabelian Hodge theory is pretty cool. I'll be
impressionistic in the following discussion. In the [previous
post](/algebraic-geometry/2020/03/21/dmod-ii.html) (see the end of the slides
for a somewhat more precise, and definitely more condensed, exposition), we
introduced this stack $$X_\lambda$$ living over $$\AA^1/\GG_m$$ whose generic
fiber is $$X_\dR$$, and whose special fiber $$X_\Dol$$ is (basically) the
quotient of $$X$$ by the completion of $$\T X$$ at the zero section. Higgs
bundles sit inside the category of perfect complexes on $$X_\Dol$$, and vector
bundles with flat connections sit inside the category of perfect complexes on
$$X_\dR$$.

Therefore, the nonabelian Hodge correspondence says that certain perfect
complexes on $$X_\Dol = (X_\lambda)_0$$ are equivalent to certain perfect
complexes on $$X_\dR = (X_\lambda)_1$$. If we think of restriction to the
special fiber of $$\AA^1/\GG_m$$ as taking a filtered object to its associated
graded, then the nonabelian Hodge correspondence can be interpreted as
determining which objects in a special class of graded objects (namely,
polystable Higgs bundles with vanishing first and second Chern classes) arise as
the associated graded of some naturally defined filtration on other objects
living on the generic fiber (namely, semisimple vector bundles with a flat
connection). Obviously, this is very vague as stated, but the hopefully it
describes the philosophy I'd like to extract from nonabelian Hodge theory. (This
philosophy was my original motivation for learning nonabelian Hodge theory.)

There's a similar situation in algebraic topology. Namely, [this
paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/1803.01804v1) introduces the notion of "synthetic
spectra". If $$E$$ is a nice homotopy commutative ring spectrum, then $$\Syn_E$$
should be thought of as the category of "formal $$E$$-based Adams-Novikov
filtrations". Although not stated this way in the paper, $$\Syn_E$$ lives over
$$\AA^1/\GG_m$$ (more precisely, it is a $$\QCoh(\AA^1/\GG_m)$$-module in stable
$$\infty$$-categories; in fact, it's monadic over $$\QCoh(\AA^1/\GG_m)$$): its
special fiber is Hovey's category of $$E_\ast E$$-comodules, while its generic
fiber is the category of spectra. One might wonder if there's some
nonabelian-Hodge-esque correspondence, describing which "graded objects" (in
this case, $$E_\ast E$$-comodules) arise as the associate graded of "objects on
the generic fiber" (in this case, the $$E$$-based ANSS filtration on certain
spectra). If $$E = \BPP$$, at least, this sounds reminiscent of the periodicity
theorem (namely, the generalized Moore spectra $$\S/(p^{i_0}, \cdots,
v_n^{i_n})$$ can be realized if $$i_0, \cdots, i_n \gg 0$$). Maybe there's
something to this analogy; I don't know.
